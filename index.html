The Regenerative Superpowers of the Axolotl

The axolotl is a unique salamander that possesses an unparalleled ability to completely regrow lost limbs, segments of its spine, and even parts of its brain and heart without any scarring. Scientists are heavily researching their massive genome to see how these cellular mechanisms might one day be applied to human regenerative medicine. To dive deeper into the science behind this creature's abilities, check out the National Institutes of Health (NIH) Axolotl Research Guide

The Social Lives of Capybaras

As the world’s largest living rodents, capybaras are famous for their incredibly calm demeanor and highly social nature. They typically live in organized family groups of 10 to 20 individuals, relying heavily on a complex system of vocalizations—such as whistles, barks, and clicks—to maintain group harmony and warn one another of predators. You can read more about their community dynamics and behavioral traits via the Oxford Academic Journal of Mammalogy.
