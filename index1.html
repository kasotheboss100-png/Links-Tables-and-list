Here are the three lists to help you understand and practice environmental conservation: 1. Precautions to Protect Nature

  • Reduce Single-Use Plasticsb: Swap out disposable water bottles, bags, and straws for reusable alternatives to keep plastic out of marine and terrestrial ecosystems.
  • Conserve Water: Turn off the tap while brushing your teeth, fix leaks promptly, and opt for short showers to protect local freshwater supplies.
Biodegradable
Capable of being decomposed naturally by bacteria or other living organisms, safely blending back into the earth.
  1. Know Your Local Rules: Check with your municipality's waste management website to learn exactly which plastics (usually indicated by numbers 1 through 7), metals, and paper products are accepted in your area.
  2. Empty and Rinse: Clean out any remaining food, liquids, or residue from plastic bottles, aluminum cans, and glass jars. Damp or dirty items cannot be processed.
